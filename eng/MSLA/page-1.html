Proposals for a Miscellaneous Statute Law Amendment Act, 2014

Proposals to correct certain anomalies, inconsistencies and errors and to deal with other matters of a non-controversial and uncomplicated nature in the Statutes of Canada and to repeal certain provisions that have expired, lapsed or otherwise ceased to have effect


MINISTER OF JUSTICE

90687(IHM-13)

Background

The Miscellaneous Statute Law Amendment Program was established in 1975. Since then, 10 Acts have been passed (1977, 1978, 1981, 1984, 1987, 1992, 1993, 1994, 1999 and 2001). The purpose of the Program is to allow minor non-controversial amendments to be made to a number of federal statutes at once, in one bill, instead of making such amendments incrementally when a particular statute is being opened for amendments of a more substantial nature.

Program Criteria

The Legislation Section of the Department of Justice is responsible for reviewing proposals. The following criteria are applied to determine whether a suggested amendment may be included in a set of proposals tabled in Parliament. The suggested amendment must meet the following criteria:

(a) not be controversial;

(b) not involve the spending of public funds;

(c) not prejudicially affect the rights of persons; or

(d) not create a new offence or subject a new class of persons to an existing offence.

The Legislation Section prepares a document entitled Proposals for a Miscellaneous Statute Law Amendment Act, (year).

Procedure

The document is tabled in the House of Commons by the Minister of Justice and referred to the Standing Committee on Justice and Human Rights. It is also tabled in the Senate and referred to the Standing Committee on Legal and Constitutional Affairs. If either of those committees considers a proposal to be controversial, the proposal is rejected.

A bill is then prepared, based on the reports of the two committees, that contains those proposals approved by both committees. The bill usually receives three readings in each House without debate since the amendments contained in the bill have already been approved by both committees.
