2001, c. 27 Immigration and Refugee Protection Act

110.  Subsection 16(3) of the English version of the Immigration and Refugee Protection Act is replaced by the following:

  • Note marginale :Evidence relating to identity

    (3) An officer may require or obtain from a permanent resident or a foreign national who is arrested, detained, subject to an examination or subject to a removal order, any evidence — photographic, fingerprint or otherwise — that may be used to establish their identity or compliance with this Act.

Immigration and Refugee Protection Act

Clause 110: This amendment would correct the English version by adding the concept of examination, making it consistent with the French version.

Existing text of subsection 16(3):

  • (3) An officer may require or obtain from a permanent resident or a foreign national who is arrested, detained or subject to a removal order, any evidence — photographic, fingerprint or otherwise — that may be used to establish their identity or compliance with this Act.

111.  Paragraph 37(1)(b) of the English version of the Act is replaced by the following:

  • (b) engaging, in the context of transnational crime, in activities such as people smuggling, trafficking in persons or laundering of money or other proceeds of crime.

Clause 111: This amendment would add a reference to the laundering of other proceeds of crime to the list of examples in the English version, making it consistent with the French version.

Relevant portion of subsection 37(1):

  • 37.  (1) A permanent resident or a foreign national is inadmissible on grounds of organized criminality for

    • ... 

    • (b) engaging, in the context of transnational crime, in activities such as people smuggling, trafficking in persons or money laundering.

112.  Subsections 63(2) and (3) of the Act are replaced by the following:

  • Note marginale :Right to appeal — visa and removal order

    (2) A foreign national who holds a permanent resident visa may appeal to the Immigration Appeal Division against a decision to make a removal order against them made under subsection 44(2) or made at an admissibility hearing.

  • Note marginale :Right to appeal removal order

    (3) A permanent resident or a protected person may appeal to the Immigration Appeal Division against a decision to make a removal order against them made under subsection 44(2) or made at an admissibility hearing.

Clause 112: This amendment would correct subsections 63(2) and (3) of the Act so that they refer to a removal order that is made under subsection 44(2) of the Act rather than at an examination. Initially, when the bill enacting the Act was introduced, subsections 44(2) and 63(2) and (3) reflected the notion that a removal order could be made following an examination — see Bill C-11, first reading version, February 21, 2001. Before it received royal assent, the bill was amended and, more specifically, the reference to an examination was removed from its subsection 44(2). Its subsections 63(2) and (3), however, were not amended as a consequence.

Existing text of subsections 63(2) and (3):

  • (2) A foreign national who holds a permanent resident visa may appeal to the Immigration Appeal Division against a decision at an examination or admissibility hearing to make a removal order against them.

  • (3) A permanent resident or a protected person may appeal to the Immigration Appeal Division against a decision at an examination or admissibility hearing to make a removal order against them.

113.  Subsection 70(1) of the English version of the Act is replaced by the following:

Note marginale :Decision binding

  • 70. (1) An officer, in examining a permanent resident or a foreign national, is bound by the decision of the Immigration Appeal Division to allow an appeal in respect of the permanent resident or foreign national.

Clause 113: This amendment would correct the English version to add a second reference to “permanent resident”, making it consistent with the French version. It is clear that this was an omission. If the provision was not meant to apply with respect to a permanent resident there would have been no reason to mention “permanent resident” at all in this sentence and “résident permanent” would not have been mentioned in the French version.

Existing text of subsection 70(1):

  • 70.  (1) An officer, in examining a permanent resident or a foreign national, is bound by the decision of the Immigration Appeal Division to allow an appeal in respect of the foreign national.

118.  Section 142 of the French version of the Act is replaced by the following:

Note marginale :Obligations

142. Les agents de la paix et les responsables immédiats d’un poste d’attente doivent, sur ordre de l’agent, exécuter les mesures — mandats et autres décisions écrites — prises au titre de la présente loi en vue de l’arrestation, de la garde ou du renvoi.

119.  Sections 196 and 197 of the French version of the Act are replaced by the following:

Note marginale :Appels

196. Malgré l’article 192, il est mis fin à l’affaire portée en appel devant la Section d’appel de l’immigration si l’intéressé est, alors qu’il ne fait pas l’objet d’un sursis prononcé au titre de l’ancienne loi, visé par la restriction du droit d’appel prévue par l’article 64 de la présente loi.

Note marginale :Sursis

197. Malgré l’article 192, l’intéressé qui fait l’objet d’un sursis prononcé au titre de l’ancienne loi et qui n’a pas respecté les conditions du sursis, est assujetti à la restriction du droit d’appel prévue par l’article 64 de la présente loi, le paragraphe 68(4) lui étant par ailleurs applicable.

196.  Despite section 192, an appeal made to the Immigration Appeal Division before the coming into force of this section shall be discontinued if the appellant has not been granted a stay under the former Act and the appeal could not have been made because of section 64 of this Act.

197.  Despite section 192, if an appellant who has been granted a stay under the former Act breaches a condition of the stay, the appellant shall be subject to the provisions of section 64 and subsection 68(4) of this Act.
