R.S., c. F-11 Financial Administration Act

Note marginale :2001, c. 4, s. 160

95.  Subsection 61(1) of the French version of the Financial Administration Act is replaced by the following:

Note marginale :Aliénation de biens publics

  • 61. (1) Sous réserve des autres lois fédérales, il ne peut être effectué de transfert, bail ni prêt portant sur des biens publics qu’en conformité avec la Loi sur les immeubles fédéraux et les biens réels fédéraux, dans le cas d’un immeuble fédéral ou d’un bien réel fédéral au sens de cette loi, et en conformité avec le paragraphe (2) dans le cas de tout autre bien public.

Financial Administration Act

Clause 95: This amendment would correct the French version by removing “de la présente loi” as this expression is unnecessary in this context.

Existing text of subsection 61(1):

  • 61.  (1) Subject to any other Act of Parliament, no transfer, lease or loan of public property shall be made except under the Federal Real Property and Federal Immovables Act in the case of federal real property or a federal immovable as defined in that Act, or under subsection (2) in the case of other public property.

R.S., c. F-14 Fisheries Act

Note marginale :R.S., c. 27 (2nd Supp.), s. 10 (Sch., item 10); 2002, c. 7, s. 173

99.  Paragraphs (c) and (d) of the definition judge in section 74 of the Fisheries Act are replaced by the following:

  • (c) in the Province of Newfoundland and Labrador, a judge of the Trial Division of the Supreme Court,

  • (d) in the Provinces of Nova Scotia, British Columbia and Prince Edward Island, Yukon and the Northwest Territories, a judge of the Supreme Court, and

Fisheries Act

Clause 99: This amendment would correct the definition “judge” with respect to Newfoundland and Labrador, as it should refer to a judge of the Trial Division of the Supreme Court rather than a judge of the Supreme Court (see: Judicature Act, R.S.N.L. 1990, c. J-4), and with respect to Prince Edward Island, as it should refer to a judge of the Supreme Court rather than a judge of the Trial Division of the Supreme Court (see: Judicature Act, R.S.P.E.I. 1988, c. J-2.1). Finally, these amendments would also change “Newfoundland” to “Newfoundland and Labrador” to take into account a constitutional amendment that changed the name of that province (see: Constitution Amendment, 2001 (Newfoundland and Labrador), SI/2001–117).

102.  Section 10 of the French version of the Health of Animals Act is replaced by the following:

Note marginale :Déplacement d’animaux malades

Health of Animals Act

Clause 102: This amendment would correct the French version by replacing “produit toxique” with the defined term “substance toxique”.

Existing text of section 10:
