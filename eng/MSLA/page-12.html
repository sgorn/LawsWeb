R.S., c. N-23 Newfoundland Additional Financial Assistance Act

137.  Sections 1 and 2 of the Newfoundland Additional Financial Assistance Act are replaced by the following:

Note marginale :Short title

1. This Act may be cited as the Newfoundland and Labrador Additional Financial Assistance Act.

Note marginale :Annual payment to Newfoundland and Labrador by way of additional financial assistance

2. In addition to all other payments, grants, subsidies and allowances payable to the Province of Newfoundland and Labrador, the Minister of Finance, on behalf of the Government of Canada, shall, out of the Consolidated Revenue Fund, cause to be paid to the Province of Newfoundland and Labrador in each and every fiscal year, until otherwise provided by any agreement in that behalf entered into between the Government of Canada and the Government of Newfoundland and Labrador, an annual amount, by way of additional financial assistance as contemplated by Term 29 of the Terms of Union of Newfoundland with Canada, of eight million dollars.

Newfoundland Additional Financial Assistance Act

Clause 137: This amendment would change “Newfoundland” to “Newfoundland and Labrador” to take into account a constitutional amendment that changed the name of that province (see: Constitution Amendment, 2001 (Newfoundland and Labrador), SI/2001-117).

Existing text of sections 1 and 2:

1.  This Act may be cited as the Newfoundland Additional Financial Assistance Act.

2.  In addition to all other payments, grants, subsidies and allowances payable to the Province of Newfoundland, the Minister of Finance, on behalf of the Government of Canada, shall, out of the Consolidated Revenue Fund, cause to be paid to the Province of Newfoundland in each and every fiscal year, until otherwise provided by any agreement in that behalf entered into between the Government of Canada and the Government of Newfoundland, an annual amount, by way of additional financial assistance as contemplated by Term 29 of the Terms of Union of Newfoundland with Canada, of eight million dollars.

1997, c. 9 Nuclear Safety and Control Act

138.  Section 80 of the Nuclear Safety and Control Act is replaced by the following:

Note marginale :Licences

80. A licence that is issued pursuant to regulations made under paragraph 9(b) of the Atomic Energy Control Act and that is in force immediately before the commencement day is deemed to have been issued under section 24 of this Act and to be in force for the remainder of the period for which it was issued under the Atomic Energy Control Act and any fees paid or payable under the AECB Cost Recovery Fees Regulations, 1996 in respect of such a licence are deemed to be paid or payable, as the case may be, under this Act.

Nuclear Safety and Control Act

Clause 138: This amendment would correct the title of the regulation by replacing “1994” with “1996” and would therefore address an issue that was raised by the Standing Joint Committee for the Scrutiny of Regulations. It would also replace “payables” with “à payer” in the French version to correct an awkward sentence structure.

Existing text of section 80:

80.  A licence that is issued pursuant to regulations made under paragraph 9(b) of the Atomic Energy Control Act and that is in force immediately before the commencement day is deemed to have been issued under section 24 of this Act and to be in force for the remainder of the period for which it was issued under the Atomic Energy Control Act and any fees paid or payable under the AECB Cost Recovery Fees Regulations, 1994 in respect of such a licence are deemed to be paid or payable, as the case may be, under this Act.
