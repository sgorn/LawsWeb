R.S., c. 1 (2nd Supp.) Customs Act

61.  Subsections 12(1) and (2) of the French version of the Customs Act are replaced by the following:

Note marginale :Déclaration

  • 12. (1) Sous réserve des autres dispositions du présent article, ainsi que des circonstances et des conditions réglementaires, toutes les marchandises importées doivent être déclarées au bureau de douane le plus proche, doté des attributions prévues à cet effet, qui soit ouvert.

  • Note marginale :Modalités

    (2) La déclaration visée au paragraphe (1) est à faire selon les modalités réglementaires de temps et de forme fixées par le gouverneur en conseil.

Customs Act

Clause 61: This amendment would correct the French version by replacing “prévues par règlement” with the defined term “réglementaire”.

Existing text of subsections 12(1) and (2):

  • 12.  (1) Subject to this section, all goods that are imported shall, except in such circumstances and subject to such conditions as may be prescribed, be reported at the nearest customs office designated for that purpose that is open for business.

  • (2) Goods shall be reported under subsection (1) at such time and in such manner as the Governor in Council may prescribe.

63.  Subsection 138(1) of the French version of the Act is replaced by the following:

Note marginale :Revendication de droits

  • 138. (1) En cas de saisie-confiscation de marchandises ou d’un moyen de transport effectuée en vertu de la présente loi ou en cas de détention d’un moyen de transport en vertu du paragraphe 97.25(2), toute personne qui, sauf si elle était en possession de l’objet au moment de la saisie ou de la détention, revendique à cet égard un droit en qualité de propriétaire, de créancier hypothécaire, de créancier privilégié ou en toute autre qualité comparable peut, dans les quatre-vingt-dix jours suivant la saisie ou la détention, demander que le ministre rende la décision visée à l’article 139.

Clause 63: This amendment would correct the French version by removing the reference to detention of goods. Only a conveyance may be detained under subsection 97.25(2).

Existing text of subsection 138(1):

  • 138.  (1) If goods or a conveyance is seized as forfeit under this Act or if a conveyance is detained under subsection 97.25(2), any person, other than the person in whose possession it was when seized or detained, who claims an interest in it as owner, mortgagee, hypothecary creditor, lien-holder or holder of any like interest may, within ninety days after the seizure or detention, apply for a decision by the Minister under section 139.

65.  The definition country in subsection 2(1) of the Customs Tariff is replaced by the following:

“country”

« pays »

country, unless the context otherwise requires, includes an external or dependent territory of a country and any other prescribed territory.

Customs Tariff

Clause 65: This amendment would remove “by regulation made by the Governor in Council” and would reposition the defined term “prescribed” to precede “territory” in the English version and would replace “prévu par règlement du gouverneur en conseil” by the defined term “réglementaire” in the French version. It would allow for the making of regulations that would determine what is meant by “territory” by referring to rules that are incorporated by reference. It would therefore address an issue that was raised by the Standing Joint Committee for the Scrutiny of Regulations.

Existing text of the definition:

country, unless the context otherwise requires, includes an external or dependent territory of a country and any other territory prescribed by regulation made by the Governor in Council.

66.  Subsection 52(2) of the French version of the Act is replaced by the following:

  • Note marginale :Renvois

    (2) Il est entendu qu’il peut être précisé, dans le règlement d’application du présent article qui incorpore par renvoi un document ou texte législatif, que celui-ci est incorporé avec ses modifications successives.

Clause 66: This amendment would amend the French version to make it consistent with the English version. The amended provision would provide that if a regulation incorporates a document or enactment by reference and ambulatory incorporation is desired, the regulation must expressly provide for this. It would therefore address an issue that was raised by the Standing Joint Committee for the Scrutiny of Regulations.

Existing text of subsection 52(2):

  • (2) For greater certainty, a regulation made under subsection (1) incorporating by reference any document or enactment may incorporate it as amended from time to time.

67.  Subparagraph 133(a)(ii) of the French version of the Act is replaced by the following:

Clause 67: This amendment would replace “nécessaires” with “convaincants” in the French version to make its language consistent with the language used in paragraph 102(a). It would therefore address an issue that was raised by the Standing Joint Committee for the Scrutiny of Regulations.
