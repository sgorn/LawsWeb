Note marginale :2002, c. 16, s. 13(1)

40.  Subsection 104(1) of the Act is replaced by the following:

Note marginale :Interim order

  • 104. (1) If an application has been made for an order under this Part, other than an interim order under section 100 or 103.3, the Tribunal, on application by the Commissioner or a person who has made an application under section 75, 76 or 77, may issue any interim order that it considers appropriate, having regard to the principles ordinarily considered by superior courts when granting interlocutory or injunctive relief.

Clause 40: This amendment would add a cross-reference to section 76 of the Competition Act. That cross-reference was added to sections 103.1 and 103.2 of that Act by the Budget Implementation Act, 2009, S.C. 2009, c. 2, but was not added to subsection 104(1), 106.1(1) or 124.2(3) (of the Competition Act). This amendment, together with the amendments in clauses 41 and 42, would correct that omission and would ensure that a consistent approach is taken in the Competition Act for private actions. This amendment would also modernize the language of the English version to conform to current drafting standards.

Existing text of subsection 104(1):

  • 104.  (1) Where an application has been made for an order under this Part, other than an interim order under section 100 or 103.3, the Tribunal, on application by the Commissioner or a person who has made an application under section 75 or 77, may issue such interim order as it considers appropriate, having regard to the principles ordinarily considered by superior courts when granting interlocutory or injunctive relief.

R.S., c. C-38 Consumer Packaging and Labelling Act

43.  Subsection 7(1) of the French version of the Consumer Packaging and Labelling Act is replaced by the following:

Note marginale :Étiquetage contenant des renseignements faux

  • 7. (1) Le fournisseur ne peut apposer sur un produit préemballé un étiquetage qui contient de l’information fausse ou trompeuse se rapportant au produit — ou pouvant raisonnablement donner cette impression —, ni vendre, importer ou annoncer un produit préemballé ainsi étiqueté.

Consumer Packaging and Labelling Act

Clause 43: This amendment would replace “produit” with the defined term “produit préemballé” in the French version, making it consistent with the English version.

Existing text of subsection 7(1):

  • 7.  (1) No dealer shall apply to any prepackaged product or sell, import into Canada or advertise any prepackaged product that has applied to it a label containing any false or misleading representation that relates to or may reasonably be regarded as relating to that product.
