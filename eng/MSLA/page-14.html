1991, c. 30 Public Sector Compensation Act

151.  Schedule I to the Public Sector Compensation Act is amended by striking out the following under the heading “OTHER PORTIONS OF THE PUBLIC SERVICE”:

  • Grain Transportation Agency

    Office du transport du grain

Public Sector Compensation Act

Clause 151: This amendment would remove the reference to the “Grain Transportation Agency” from Schedule I to the Act since that Agency no longer exists. The Act under which it was established, the Western Grain Transportation Act, R.S., c. W-8, was repealed by the Budget Implementation Act, 1995, S.C. 1995, c. 17, s. 26.

R.S., c. 32 (4th Supp.) Railway Safety Act

Note marginale :1992, c. 51, s. 61

152.  Paragraphs (a.1) and (b) of the definition superior court in subsection 4(1) of the Railway Safety Act are replaced by the following:

  • (a.1) in the Province of Newfoundland and Labrador, the Trial Division of the Supreme Court of the Province,

  • (b) in the Provinces of Nova Scotia, British Columbia and Prince Edward Island, the Supreme Court of the Province,

Railway Safety Act

Clause 152: This amendment would change “Newfoundland” to “Newfoundland and Labrador” to take into account a constitutional amendment that changed the name of that province (see: Constitution Amendment, 2001 (Newfoundland and Labrador), SI/2001-117). It would also correct the reference to the court in Prince Edward Island. Because of the reorganization of that court, the reference should be to the Supreme Court rather than the Trial Division of the Supreme Court (see: Judicature Act, R.S.P.E.I. 1988, c. J-2.1).

1993, c. 37 Seized Property Management Act

Note marginale :2001, c. 41, s. 108

154.  Subsection 5(3) of the Seized Property Management Act is repealed.

Seized Property Management Act

Clause 154: This amendment would repeal subsection 5(3), which repeats the obligation already found in subsection 5(1).

Existing text of subsection 5(3):

  • (3) Every person who has control of any property that is subject to a management order issued under subsection 83.13(2) of the Criminal Code shall, as soon as practicable after the order is issued, transfer the control of the property to the Minister, except for any property, or any part of the property, that is needed as evidence or is necessary for the purposes of an investigation.

2002, c. 29 Species at Risk Act

155.  Sections 98 and 99 of the English version of the Species at Risk Act are replaced by the following:

Note marginale :Officers, etc., of corporations

98. If a corporation commits an offence, any officer, director or agent or mandatary of the corporation who directed, authorized, assented to, or acquiesced or participated in, the commission of the offence is a party to and guilty of the offence and is liable on conviction to the punishment provided for the offence, whether or not the corporation has been prosecuted or convicted.

Note marginale :Offences by employees or agents or mandataries

99. In any prosecution for an offence, the accused may be convicted of the offence if it is established that it was committed by an employee or an agent or mandatary of the accused, whether or not the employee, agent or mandatary has been prosecuted for the offence.

Species at Risk Act

Clause 155: This amendment would correct a typographical error in the English version — the correct word is “mandatary”.

Existing text of sections 98 and 99:

98.  If a corporation commits an offence, any officer, director, agent or mandatory of the corporation who directed, authorized, assented to, or acquiesced or participated in, the commission of the offence is a party to and guilty of the offence and is liable on conviction to the punishment provided for the offence, whether or not the corporation has been prosecuted or convicted.

99.  In any prosecution for an offence, the accused may be convicted of the offence if it is established that it was committed by an employee, agent or mandatory of the accused, whether or not the employee, agent or mandatory has been prosecuted for the offence.

Note marginale :2009, c. 9, s. 26

158.  Section 27.3 of the French version of the Transportation of Dangerous Goods Act, 1992 is replaced by the following:

Note marginale :Mesure de sûreté prise par le sous-ministre autorisé par le ministre

  • 27.3 (1) Le ministre peut, sous réserve des restrictions et conditions qu’il précise, autoriser le sous-ministre des Transports à prendre des mesures de sûreté dans les cas où celui-ci estime que de telles mesures sont immédiatement requises pour la sécurité publique et où les conditions prévues aux alinéas 27.2(2)a) et b) sont réunies.

  • Note marginale :Période de validité

    (2) La mesure de sûreté entre en vigueur dès sa prise et le demeure pendant quatre-vingt-dix jours, à moins que le ministre ou le sous-ministre ne l’abroge plus tôt.

Transportation of Dangerous Goods Act, 1992

Clause 158: This amendment would correct terminological errors in the French version.

Existing text of section 27.3:

  • 27.3  (1) The Minister may authorize his or her deputy to make, subject to any restrictions or conditions that the Minister specifies, security measures whenever the deputy is of the opinion that the measures are immediately required for public safety, provided that the conditions in paragraphs 27.2(2)(a) and (b) are met.

  • (2) The security measure comes into force immediately when it is made but ceases to have force 90 days after the day on which it is made unless the Minister or his or her deputy repeals it earlier.

Note marginale :2005, c. 21, s. 113(1)

161.  Subsection 34(1) of the French version of the Act is replaced by the following:

Note marginale :Nouvelle demande

  • 34. (1) En cas de refus d’une compensation visée par la Loi sur les pensions ou de l’indemnité d’invalidité, de l’indemnité de décès, de l’allocation vestimentaire ou de l’indemnité de captivité visées par la partie 3 de la Loi sur les mesures de réinsertion et d’indemnisation des militaires et vétérans des Forces canadiennes, une personne peut, après avoir épuisé les recours en révision et en appel prévus par la présente loi, adresser au Tribunal une demande d’allocation de commisération.

Clause 161: This amendment would amend the French version so that the reference to “compensations visées par la Loi sur les pensions” is drafted in the singular, making it consistent with such references in the Pension Act.

Existing text of subsection 34(1):

  • 34.  (1) A person who has been refused an award under the Pension Act, or a disability award, a death benefit, a clothing allowance or a detention benefit under Part 3 of the Canadian Forces Members and Veterans Re-establishment and Compensation Act, and who has exhausted all procedures for review and appeal under this Act may apply to the Board for a compassionate award.
