Note marginale :1992, c. 51, s. 32(1)

  • 45.  (1) The definition court of appeal in section 2 of the Criminal Code is replaced by the following:

    “court of appeal”

    « cour d’appel »

    court of appeal means, in all provinces, the Court of Appeal.

    Criminal Code

    Clause 45: (1) This amendment would correct the reference to the court in Prince Edward Island. Because of the reorganization of that court, the reference should be to the Court of Appeal rather than the Appeal Division of the Supreme Court (see: Judicature Act, R.S.P.E.I. 1988, c. J-2.1). In addition, the substance of paragraphs (a) and (b) would be merged since in all provinces, “court of appeal” refers to the Court of Appeal of the province in question.

    Existing text of the definition:

    court of appeal means

    • (a) in the Province of Prince Edward Island, the Appeal Division of the Supreme Court, and

    • (b) in all other provinces, the Court of Appeal;

  • Note marginale :1992, c. 51, s. 32(3)

    (2) Paragraph (c) of the definition superior court of criminal jurisdiction in section 2 of the Act is replaced by the following:

    • (c) in the Provinces of Nova Scotia, British Columbia and Prince Edward Island, the Court of Appeal or the Supreme Court,

  • Note marginale :1992, c. 51, s. 32(4); 1999, c. 3, s. 25; 2002, c. 7, s. 137(2)

    (3) Paragraphs (e) to (h) of the definition superior court of criminal jurisdiction in section 2 of the Act are replaced by the following:

    • (e) in the Province of Newfoundland and Labrador, Yukon and the Northwest Territories, the Supreme Court, and

    • (f) in Nunavut, the Nunavut Court of Justice;

    (2) and (3) These amendments would correct the references to the courts in Prince Edward Island and Newfoundland and Labrador. In Prince Edward Island, the former Supreme Court has been continued as the Court of Appeal and the Supreme Court. Consequently, with respect to Prince Edward Island, the provision should refer to both the Court of Appeal and the Supreme Court (see: Judicature Act, R.S.P.E.I. 1988, c. J-2.1). In Newfoundland and Labrador, the reference should be to the Supreme Court rather than to the Supreme Court and the Court of Appeal (see: Judicature Act, R.S.N.L. 1990, c. J-4). In addition, the content of several paragraphs of this definition has been rearranged and two paragraphs have been repealed in order to take into account these amendments and comply with current drafting standards. Finally, these amendments would also change “Newfoundland” to “Newfoundland and Labrador” to take into account a constitutional amendment that changed the name of that province (see: Constitution Amendment, 2001 (Newfoundland and Labrador), SI/2001-117).
